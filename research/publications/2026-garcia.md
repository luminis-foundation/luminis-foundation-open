# Bridging Mycelium-Inspired Decentralized Computing and Symbiotic Plant–Fungal–AI Bio-Hybrid Systems in Northern New Mexico's High-Desert Ecosystems

**Authors:** Garcia, C.

**Year:** 2026

**DOI:** [10.5281/zenodo.20143391](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20143391)

**Repository:** Zenodo

## Abstract

This paper explores the intersection of mycelium-inspired decentralized computing architectures and symbiotic plant–fungal–AI bio-hybrid systems, situated in the high-desert ecosystems of Northern New Mexico. Drawing on fungal electrophysiology, mycorrhizal ecology, and TinyML edge computing, we propose a framework for sovereign, ecologically-situated artificial intelligence that operates on the rhythms and constraints of a specific landscape — the Pecos watershed.

## Key Topics

- Mycelium-inspired network topologies for decentralized AI
- TinyML sensor integration with living fungal substrates
- Privacy-preserving federated learning across bio-hybrid nodes
- Field deployment framework for the Pecos watershed (Section 10.5)
- Ethical and governance considerations for bio-hybrid systems
