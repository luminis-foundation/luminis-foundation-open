# Field Deployment

Luminis Foundation's field work is centered in the Pecos watershed of Northern New Mexico — a high-desert landscape of piñon-juniper woodlands, seasonal arroyos, and mycorrhizal networks that has informed our research from the beginning.

## Why the Pecos Watershed

The Pecos watershed provides:
- **Existing mycorrhizal networks** connecting piñon pine, juniper, and associated flora
- **Extreme environmental gradients** (elevation, temperature, moisture) that stress-test edge computing systems
- **Cultural and ecological significance** grounding the work in place and community
- **Practical access** for iterative deployment and long-term monitoring

## Deployment Approach

Our field deployment follows Section 10.5 of the [foundational paper](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20143391):

1. **Site characterization** — Soil composition, moisture profiles, existing fungal networks
2. **Sensor node placement** — TinyML-equipped nodes positioned along mycorrhizal pathways
3. **Bio-hybrid integration** — Interfacing electronic sensors with living fungal substrates
4. **Network topology** — Mycelium-inspired decentralized routing between nodes
5. **Data collection** — Longitudinal ecological and system performance monitoring
