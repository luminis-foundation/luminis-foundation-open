# Mission

## Purpose

Luminis Foundation exists to advance scientific research, education, and public-benefit technological development focused on decentralized artificial intelligence, environmental sustainability, and privacy-preserving digital systems that promote the long-term well-being of humanity and the natural world.

## What We Stand For

- **Sovereignty over dependence** — Technology that belongs to its users and their communities
- **Privacy over surveillance** — Systems that protect identity and agency by design
- **Regeneration over extraction** — Infrastructure aligned with ecological health
- **Stewardship over exploitation** — Long-term thinking over short-term gain
- **Decentralization over concentrated control** — Distributed power, distributed resilience

## Why This Matters

Modern technological systems are increasingly centralized, extractive, and dependent on surveillance-based models. Our work offers an alternative grounded in a specific place — the Pecos watershed of Northern New Mexico — where mycelial networks, seasonal rhythms, and community self-determination inform how we design and deploy AI systems.

Our [foundational research](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20143391) demonstrates that decentralized computing architectures inspired by fungal networks can operate effectively at the edge, without dependence on centralized cloud infrastructure.

## Research Focus

- Mycelium-inspired decentralized computing
- Bio-hybrid systems (plant–fungal–AI integration)
- TinyML and edge-deployed sensor networks
- Privacy-preserving federated learning
- Ecological monitoring and regenerative technology
